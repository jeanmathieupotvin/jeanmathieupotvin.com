About me

Actuary (ac-tu-ar-y)

A person who compiles and analyzes statistics and uses them to calculate insurance risks and premiums (Oxford Dictionary).

This definition is well suited to traditional actuarial business but fails to define modern actuarial science. The latter lies at the intersection of mathematics, statistics, machine learning, computer science, finance and other related fields such as physics. To price a risk you must first understand it. To understand it, you need a simplified representation of the underlying stochastic phenomenon and thus, you need a model. Modern models usually require excellent developer skills. A modern actuary has a unique skillset along with exceptional analysis skills.

My end-goal

I seek to become the perfect hybrid between a developer and a research professional. I want to deliver analytical solutions along with a modern and robust implementation.
